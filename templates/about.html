About the Aramaic Root Atlas

The Aramaic Root Atlas is a cross-corpus triliteral root explorer spanning centuries of Aramaic literary history. It allows scholars to trace a single Semitic root from the Peshitta through Biblical Aramaic, Targum, and Syriac patristic literature — with Hebrew, Arabic, and Greek cognates visible at every layer.

Corpora

  • Peshitta NT — The Syriac New Testament (7,957 verses)
  • Peshitta OT — Selected Old Testament books: Psalms, Proverbs, Isaiah, Ezekiel (5,930 verses)
  • Biblical Aramaic, Targum Onkelos, Ephrem's Hymns — Coming soon

How It Works

The engine extracts triliteral consonantal roots from unvocalized Syriac text using morphological analysis (prefix/suffix stripping) and a known-root dictionary. Roots are normalized to a shared Latin key (e.g., k-t-b) enabling cross-script comparison with Hebrew and Arabic cognates.
